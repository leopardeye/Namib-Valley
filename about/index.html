Welcome to
Namib's Valley of a Thousand Hills

Crafting Tranquil Escapes in the Heart of Namibia

Nestled on elevated ground overlooking the Gaub Valley, Namib's Valley of a Thousand Hills emerges as a haven where nature's embrace meets the charm of Namibia. Our story begins with a passion for creating tranquil escapes in the heart of this mesmerising landscape.

We are delighted to offer Namib's Valley Lodge as the ideal stopover for travellers between Windhoek, Swakopmund, and Sossusvlei. Our lodge is a shining example of sustainable living amid the Namib Desert's remarkable beauty. We take great pride in providing more than just accommodation for our guests but also a unique and eco-friendly experience that leaves a lasting impression.

Our accommodations, including rustic bungalows and family-friendly options, seamlessly blend with the natural surroundings, offering a genuine desert experience. The main lodge, with its fully functioning restaurant, bar, infinity pool, and lounge, becomes a hub for relaxation after days of exploration.

More than a destination, Namib's Valley is a commitment to responsible tourism. Our electricity, sourced sustainably from solar and wind power, reflects our dedication to eco-friendly practices. Fresh vegetables from local markets support nearby businesses and enhance the authenticity of your dining experience.

As you embark on your Namibian journey, consider Namib's Valley of a Thousand Hills not just as a place to rest but as a unique chapter in your adventure, where tranquillity meets the allure of the Namib Desert.

Geology

Unraveling the Geological Mysteries of the Gaub Formation

The Gaub Formation, a crucial geological entity in the southern margin zone of the Damara Orogen, reveals the story of Earth's ancient history. Consisting mainly of dark greenish-grey quartz-chlorite-biotite-sericite schist, this formation unravels its secrets through meticulous examination.

Dated to approximately 1227 million years ago (Ma), the Gaub Formation is a testament to the dynamic geological processes that shaped the region. Characterised by diverse lithologies, including schists, quartzites, and marbles, it signifies a snapshot of Earth's past and hints at the tectonic forces that once moulded its landscapes.

The Gaub Valley Formation, an integral part of the broader Gaub Formation, showcases a stratigraphic profile marked by clast-supported conglomerates and polymict conglomeratic rocks. These formations, notably exposed south of the Gamsberg massif, play a crucial role in deciphering the region's geological evolution.

Adjacent to the Gaub Formation, the Gamsberg Granitic Suite and Rostock Nappe Complex add layers to the geological narrative. The Klein Gamsberg Granite, a prominent member of the Gamsberg Suite, intrudes the Palaeoproterozoic Gaub Valley and Elim Formations. This intrusion, dated around 1226 Ma, provides insights into the magmatic activities that influenced the surrounding geological features.

The Rostock Nappe Complex, occurring in the Natas and Rostock areas, exhibits a double D, antiformal structure. Damaran units within this complex, including Kamtsas, Duruchaus, Berghof, Blaukrans, Hakosberg, and Waldburg Formations, contribute to the geological mosaic of the region. The Rostock Dome, a stacked antiformal duplex, is a geological testimony to the forces that shaped its present form.

In summary, with its distinctive lithological composition and age, the Gaub Formation is a crucial player in the geological theatre of the Damara Orogen. Its interaction with neighbouring formations, such as the Gamsberg Granitic Suite and Rostock Nappe Complex, provides a rich tapestry for scientists and researchers aiming to unravel the geological history of this fascinating region.

Biodiversity

A Symphony of Fauna and Flora in Namibia's Heart

Namib's Valley of a Thousand Hills is a canvas painted with the vibrant hues of Namibia's unique biodiversity. The fauna and flora thrive harmoniously, creating a symphony of life amidst the desert.

Majestic Kudu, Oryx, and Springbuck gracefully roam, while the Bat-eared fox and Cape grey mongoose add an air of mystery. Witness the resilient dance of the Dancing Spider, the Namaqua rock agama's colour palette, and the Spiny-tailed lizard's deceptive tactics.

Beneath the vast Namibian skies, large raptors like the Martial eagle and Verreaux's eagle share the airspace with smaller birds of prey, such as the Rock kestrel and Greater kestrel. The desert-adapted Namaqua sandgrouse and Grey-winged francolin navigate the arid landscape while the Pale chanting goshawk and Cinnamon-breasted bee-eater grace the scene. Layers of Time

A Tapestry of Stories:
From Indigenous Cultures to Colonial Intricacies

Explore the historical richness woven into Namib's Valley, a land where each layer tells a story spanning millennia. Inhabited for centuries by indigenous Khoisan hunter-gatherers, the region bears witness to their presence through ancient rock paintings and engravings. Around 2000 ago, Bantu-speaking groups introduced livestock herding and agriculture, shaping the cultural landscape.

The colonial era left indelible marks as Germany established Swakopmund and the vital railway connecting Windhoek. However, this period is also marked by resistance, leading to the Herero and Namaqua genocide (1904-1907) and the subsequent Battle of Swakopmund during World War I.

Today, the Gaub Pass and its surroundings draw curious visitors seeking scenic beauty and wildlife and a connection to Namibia's intricate past. Ongoing conservation endeavours strive to safeguard the fragile Namib Desert ecosystem, preserving the tales imprinted in the sands of time.
